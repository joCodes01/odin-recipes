Macarons

Macaron recipe

These Macarons are a special treat and sure to make a gathering that little bit extra special.

Stack these fancy little treats up, present them individually for a special desert or box them up as a sweet treat for someone special.

Ingredients

    Macarons

  • 1 ¾ cups powdered sugar
  • 1 cup almond flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 3 egg whites (room temp)
  • ¼ cup granulated sugar
  • 1 ½ cups all purpose flour
  • ½ tsp vanilla extract
  • ½ tsp baking powder
  • 2 drops pink gel food colouring

    Buttercream

  • 1 cup unsalted butter
  • 3 cups powdered sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 3 tbsp heavy cream

Steps

  1. Mix the flour

    Combine the powdered sugar, almond flour and ½ teaspoon of salt. mix in food processor on low speed until powder is extra fine then sift the mixture into a large bowl.

  2. Beat the mixture

    In another large bowl beat the egg whites and ½ teaspoon of salt with an electric hand mixer until peaks begin to form. Gradually add the sugar and continue to beat until the peaks become stiff. Add the vanilla extract and food colouring and beat until just combined

  3. Fold the mixture

    Add ⅓ of the sifted flour mixture and fold in until combined, then add the next ⅓ and fold in and then the final ⅓. Fold the mixture slowly until it falls from the spatula in ribbons. If you have time then leave the mixture overnight for the best texture results. If not then go onto the next step when you are ready.

  4. Pipe the mixture

    Transfer the mixture into a piping bag with a round tip. Pipe 1 ½ circles spacing 1 inch apart on a parchment lined baking sheet. Tap the baking sheet to release any bubbles and let stand for 30 minutes to 1 hour until dry to the touch

  5. Bake

    After preheating the oven to 150Â°C, bake for 17 minutes until the lower part is well risen and the macarons don't stick to the parchment paper then cool on a wire rack before filling.

  6. Make the buttercream

    In a large bowl, beat the butter with a mixer until light and fluffy then sift in the powdered sugar and beat until fully combined. Add the vanilla and beat to combine then add the cream 1 tbsp at a time and beat to combine until the desired consistency is achieved.

  7. Pipe the buttercream

    Transfer the buttercream to a piping bag with a round tip and add a squirt of buttercream to one macaroon side. Close this with another macaron to create a little sandwich. Repeat with the rest of the macarons and buttercream.

  8. Pipe the buttercream

    Transfer the buttercream to a piping bag with a round tip and add a squirt of buttercream to one macaroon side. Close this with another macaron to create a little sandwich. Repeat with the rest of the macarons and buttercream.

  9. Let them bloom

    Put the macarons in a sealed container and into the fridge for at least 2 hours but ideally overnight for the best texture.
